Transportation Revolution

In 2014 320,000 new electric vehicles were registered around the world, in 2019 that number was 2.3 million by 2025 global Electric vehicle sales are projected to surpass 10 million and by 2050 the majority of automobiles will be electric. Gas stations will disappear and be replaced with at home charging stations. Automobile will become driverless as of now Tesla vehicles already have autopilot features.

  • Self Driving Cars: Google have invested billions of dollars into self-driving technology currently it's expected that fully autonomous level 5 vehicles will roll out to consumers in the late to mid 2020s and become commonplace in the 2030s.By 2050 people will be hopping into cars with no steering wheel thousands of autonomous vans and semi trucks will travel across country.
  • DronesDelivering packages and shipments with no human interaction drones will do the same for shorter distances by 2050 swarms of drones will be delivering small packages from floating or vertical warehouses.There will also be surveillance in security drones along with the drones for construction entertainment and agriculture.
  • E-VehicleEVIE tolls or electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft will provide urban transportation. uber wants to make aerial ride-sharing a reality there's also Airbus who have their drone like Airbus pop-up concept the Humber Airbus and other companies succeed we could see widespread adoption of the technology by 2050.
  • HyperloopsElon Musk's boring company is digging small experimental tunnels under cities. If musk can significantly cut down boring costs, by 2050 cities will have thousands of tunnels underneath them. Hyperloop will revolutionize long distance land transportation.
  • Super Sonic planesAirplanes will remain dominant with improved batteries ,we could see electric airliners take to the skies. In addition several companies are working to bring back supersonic aircraft. These aircraft are certainly not airliners but small-scale jets meant to carry only 10 to 100 people.
